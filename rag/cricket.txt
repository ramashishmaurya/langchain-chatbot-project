Cricket is one of the most popular sports in the world, especially in countries like India, Australia, and England. It is played between two teams of eleven players each.

The game is played on a circular or oval field with a rectangular pitch in the center. Each team takes turns to bat and bowl.

Sachin Tendulkar is known as the "God of Cricket" and is one of the greatest batsmen of all time. He has scored 100 international centuries.

Virat Kohli is a modern-day cricket legend and former captain of the Indian cricket team. He is known for his aggressive batting style and consistency.

MS Dhoni is one of the most successful captains in cricket history. He led India to victory in the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a professional T20 cricket league in India. It features city-based franchise teams like Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings.

T20 cricket is the shortest format of the game, where each team plays 20 overs. It is fast-paced and highly entertaining.

ODI (One Day International) cricket consists of 50 overs per team. It is a balance between Test cricket and T20 cricket.

Test cricket is the longest format and can last up to five days. It is considered the purest form of the game.

Bowling styles in cricket include fast bowling, swing bowling, and spin bowling. Spin bowlers like Shane Warne and Muttiah Muralitharan are famous for their skills.

Batting techniques include defensive shots, drives, pulls, and cuts. A good batsman needs timing, footwork, and concentration.

Fielding is also an important part of cricket. Great fielders can change the outcome of a match with their catches and run-outs.

The Ashes is a historic Test cricket series played between England and Australia.

The ICC (International Cricket Council) is the global governing body of cricket.

Cricket World Cup is one of the biggest tournaments in the sport, held every four years.

India won the 2011 Cricket World Cup under the captaincy of MS Dhoni.
